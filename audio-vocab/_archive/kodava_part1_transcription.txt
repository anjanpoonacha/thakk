Learning the question and answer sentences. The first word we will be looking at is what.
What is your name? **niida pedha entha?** **niida** means your, **pedha** means name, and **entha** means what.
My name is Rahul. **naada pedha** Rahul. Here **naada** means my and **pedha** means name.

What are you doing? **entha maadiyand uLLiya?** Here **entha** means what, **maadiyand** means doing. **uLLiya** is used for like present tense.
I am having coffee. **naan kaapi kudchiyand uLL.** **naan** means I, **kaapi** means coffee, **kudchiyand** is like drinking, **uLL** is used because this is a present tense.
What were you doing? **entha maadiyand injiiyaa?** Here **entha** means what, **maadiyand** means doing. Here we use **injiiyaa**. In the previous sentence we used **uLLiya** because it was a present tense. Here we use **injiiyaa** because it is a past tense.
I had been out. **naan porme poayith injiye.** **naan** means I, **porme** means out, **poayith** is like, **poa** is like going. Uh here we use **injiye** because this is the, this is we're telling in past tense. Previously we used **uLL**.
What is her name? **avada pedha entha?** Here **ava** means her. **ava** refers to her or she. **pedha** is name and **entha** is what.
Her name is Sakshi. **avada pedha** Sakshi. We use **ava** because we refer to like girl, her or she.
What is his name? **ããwuNda pedha entha?** Here **ããwu** refers to like he, him or his. It is used for like boys. Uh **pedha** means name and **entha** means what.
His name is Manish. Again we use **ããwu** because his is **ããwu**. **pedha** refers to name.

Why are you scared? **nii'k ennang poadi?** **nii'k** means like for you. **ennang** means why and **poadi** means scared.
