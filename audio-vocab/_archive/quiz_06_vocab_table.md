# Kodava Padipu Quiz 6 — Kodava Family Relationships (Chaache)

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Je1JO42f7RI

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Father's youngest brother | kunyappa | ningada appaNda eLiya thammaN = your father's youngest brother |
| Mother's immediate younger brother | boji maamu / boji maama | ningada ammaNda dhandane thammaN = your mother's immediate younger brother |
| Brother's wife's brother (brother-in-law) | baava / machchiNa | ningada aNNaNda poNNura thammaN = your brother's wife's brother |
| Son-in-law (how wife's parents refer to him) | maima | oar poNNaaLira vaDiya avada appa avvangku = a man as referred to by his wife's parents |
| Paternal grandfather / elder paternal clansman | thaatha | ningada kunjira balyappaNda pappu = your child's paternal uncle's father |
| Agnatic paternal uncle (elder male clansman) | ayya | How a child addresses an elder male manekaara (clansman) on father's side |
| Mother-in-law's sister | maami | ningada maamiyira akku illengi thange = your mother-in-law's sister |
| Husband's older brother's wife | akka / akkeya | ningada vaDiyanDa aNNaNda poNNu = your husband's older brother's wife |
| Co-mother-in-law (mother-in-law's term for one's mother) | momma | ningada maami ningada avvangku = your mother-in-law addresses your mother |
| Nephew / Niece | kunyi | ningada akkayeNda illengi aNNaNda kunyi = your sister's or brother's child |
