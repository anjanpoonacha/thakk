# Quiz 17 — Kodava Padipu Vocab Table

**Topic:** Kaveri river traditions, Kodava festivals and ceremonies, Puttari rituals

## Kaveri River and Festival Knowledge

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Month in which Kaveri Changrandi is observed | Toleyaar ondu | first month of the Kodava calendar |
| The sacred name for flowing to Talakaveri | Kiri Changrandi | refers to the ceremonial visit to Talakaveri source |
| The figure associated with Kaveri Purana who protected the river | Balambeeri | legendary protector in Kaveri tradition |
| Worship ceremony with coconut/flowers at river | KaNi pooje | kaNi = offerings; pooje = worship ritual |

## Nerekattu — Items Tied Together (offering bundle)

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Banana leaf | kummi ele | kummi = tender; ele = leaf |
| Jack fruit leaf | geke ele | geke = jackfruit; ele = leaf |
| Areca leaf | aalu ele | aalu = areca; ele = leaf |
| Tender coconut | enjooLi kodi | enjooLi = tender; kodi = coconut |
| Tied bundle on bamboo | achinaaru baLLilu kattuva | achina = specific vine; baLLi = bamboo |

## Puttari Ceremonies

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Pre-Puttari gathering for kolaat dance | Iidu | community gathering week before harvest festival |
| Ritual powder mixture placed on new paddy | BerumbuTTu | berumbu = a plant; TuTTu = application; placed on paddy stalks at Kailpodh |

## Mane Paado — House Singing Ritual

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Visiting homes to sing after Puttari | Mane paado | mane = house; paado = singing |

## ELakki Puttu — Seven Ingredients

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Rice flour | akki podi | akki = rice; podi = flour/powder |
| Jaggery | aLLu | unrefined cane sugar |
| Dry ginger rice | shunti chooru | shunti = dry ginger; chooru = cooked rice |
| Bitter gourd rice | kaipake chooru | kaipake = bitter gourd; chooru = rice |
| Coconut rice | tenga chooru | tenga = coconut; chooru = rice |
| Banana | baaLe | ripe banana fruit |
| Seven small stones | eLu cheriya kallu | eLu = seven; cheriya = small; kallu = stones |
