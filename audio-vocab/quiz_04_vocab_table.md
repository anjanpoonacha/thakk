# Kodava Padipu Quiz 4 — Kodava Games, Culture & Traditions

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=alJT16SVA50

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| A traditional game played with two sticks (large and small) | thaara kaLi | Also known as chinni thaar; played with a large stick hitting a small one |
| Work / profession / job | chaakri | udyoga = profession (Sanskrit); chaakri = Kodava word for one's job |
| Lid of a bamboo basket (for laying a newborn) | thaDpe | Traditionally, newborns were laid on this lid for the first 12 days |
| Auspicious flower used to worship weapons at Kailpodh | thook' puu | Used to decorate weapons during the Kailpodh festival |
| A dish made from broken rice | paapuTTu | Traditional Kodava dish made from broken/coarse rice (thari) |
| Neighboring districts of Kodagu within Karnataka | Dakshina Kannada, Hassan, Mysuru | Karnataka'k kuuDuna KoDugura nereya jille |
| A seat in a traditional house reserved for elders | aimara | Found in every ainmane; kayyaale'l periyangak'k aLpakoLLo jaaga |
| First Indian woman to join the Indian Foreign Service (IFS) | C. B. Muthamma | Famous personality from Kodagu |
| Cauldron / large pot for heating water | kala | gudda'thl kuLippa niir kaacho'k upayogchido paathra |
| Part of the ear where an earring is pierced | kemi thaTTu | kemi = ear; thaTTu = the piercing spot/lobe |
