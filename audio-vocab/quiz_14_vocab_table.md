# Quiz 14 — Kodava Padipu Vocabulary Table

This quiz covers the Kodava Takk names for various traditional utensils and vessels commonly found in a Kodava kitchen. It explains the specific use for each item, from cooking and frying to preparing special dishes.

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| A traditional blade used to grate coconut or cut meat. | paleya katti | Used for grating coconut (`thenge thorundooku`) or cutting meat (`erchi murippakku`). |
| A vessel for heating oil. | eNNe charkku | Used for heating oil (`eNNe kaachokku`). |
| A press for making nooputtu (rice noodles). | noopuTTu vara | Used to make `noopuTTu` (`noopuTTu maaduwakku`). |
| A handheld chopper for curry ingredients. | kai kaNe | Used for chopping curry ingredients (`kari kothokku`). `kai` means hand. |
| Plate. | thaLiya | A general term for a plate. |
| A large pot for fetching and boiling water. | baacharate | Used to fetch and boil water (`niiru kothookku`). |
| Frying pan / Wok. | chiin chaTTi | Used for frying meat (`erchi barpakku`) or eggs (`muTTe barpakku`). |
| A small pan for tempering spices (tadka). | baaluruLi | Used for tempering spices for a curry (`karikku aggaraNe maaDakku`). |
| A pot for storing leftover rice gruel (kanji). | kanjikala | A pot (`paathra`) to keep leftover rice gruel (`kuuL'ra kanji`). |
| A griddle for making dosa. | doase kallu | Used for making dosa (`doase bookuvakku`). `kallu` means stone. |
