# Quiz 07 — Kodava Vocabulary
> **Source:** quiz_07.mp3
> **Format:** 10-question Kodava general knowledge quiz

| English | Kodava Takk | Kannada Script | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Studies/Learning | Padippu | ಪಡಿಪು | Refers to studies or learning, as in the group name "Kodava Padippu". |
| Team | tanda | ತಂಡ | A group or team. |
| Presentation | prastuthi | ಪ್ರಸ್ತುತಿ | A presentation or production, a loanword from Kannada. |
| Mushroom | Kummu | ಕುಮ್ಮು | The general Kodava word for a mushroom. |
| State/Kingdom | rajya | ರಾಜ್ಯ | A state or kingdom, used in the context of Kodagu's history. |
| When did it merge? | ekka koodiye | ಎಕ್ಕ ಕೂಡಿಯೇ | A question phrase meaning "when did it join or merge?". |
| Kaveri River | Kaaloodhi | ಕಾಲೋದಿ | The Kodava name for the sacred river Kaveri. |
| Fields | boLera | ಬೊಳೆಯರ | Refers to agricultural fields, typically wet paddy fields. |
| Post/Stick | koalu | ಕೋಲು | A stick, pole, or post. |
| Creeper/Vine | baLLi | ಬಳ್ಳಿ | A climbing plant or vine. |
| Wrapped Post | Bothu | ಬೊತ್ತು | A sacred, creeper-wrapped post placed in fields for Kaveri Sankramana. |
| Kodava Month (Aug-Sep) | Chinyaru | ಚಿನ್ಯಾರ | A month in the Kodava calendar, when Kailpodhu is celebrated. |
| On the 18th day | padinettanga | ಪದಿನೆಟ್ಟಾಂಗ | A term specifying the 18th day of a month. |
| Festival | namme | ನಮ್ಮೆ | The general Kodava word for a festival or celebration. |
| Festival of Arms | Kailpodhu | ಕೈಲ್ ಪೋದು | A major Kodava festival celebrating arms and the end of the farming season. |
| First | aadhya | ಆದ್ಯ | Means "first" or "initial". |
| TV Serial | dharavahi | ಧಾರಾವಾಹಿ | A loanword for a television serial or series. |
| Ancestral Home | Ainumane | ಐನುಮನೆ | The name of the first Kodava TV serial; also means ancestral family home. |
| How many? | echaka | ಎಚ್ಚಕ | A question word meaning "how many?". |
| Region/Territory | naadu | ನಾಡ್ | A traditional administrative region or territory within Kodagu. |
| Thirty-five (35) | Muppathanji | ಮುಪ್ಪತಂಜಿ | The Kodava word for the number thirty-five. |
| Fig Fruit | Athi pannu | ಅತ್ತಿ ಪಣ್ಣ್ | The Kodava term for fig fruit; `pannu` means fruit. |
| Main Door | edikke padi | ಎದಿಕೆ ಪಡಿ | The main entrance or front door of a traditional Kodava house. |
| To the direction | dikkukku | ದಿಕ್ಕುಕ್ | A word indicating direction. |
| East | Kerkku | ಕೇರ್ಕ್ | The cardinal direction East. |
| Eastern Door | kerkkora padi | ಕೇರ್ಕೊರ ಪಡಿ | Refers specifically to the eastern-facing door of a house. |
| Compound/Property | kaLathra | ಕಳತ್ರ | The compound, yard, or grounds surrounding a house. |
| Stones | kallulu | ಕಲ್ಲುಲ್ | The plural form of `kallu` (stone). |
| Built | katti | ಕಟ್ಟಿ | Means "built" or "constructed". |
| Stone Compound Wall | Kakkattu | ಕಕ್ಕಟ್ಟು | A traditional dry-stone wall built with jungle stones around a property. |
| Clan/Family | okka | ಒಕ್ಕ | A patrilineal clan or large joint family group in Kodava society. |
| Hereditary Temple Servants | thakkangaLu | ತಕ್ಕಂಗಳು | Hereditary spokespersons or functionaries for a temple or deity. |
| Parandanda (Clan Name) | Parandanda | ಪರಂದಂಡ | The name of the specific clan that serves as `devathakkas` for Igguthappa temple. |
| Please | dayavittu | ದಯವಿಟ್ಟು | A polite term for "please," borrowed from Kannada. |
| And then/Also | pinne | ಪಿನ್ನೇ | A conjunction meaning "and then" or "also". |
| Thank you | dhanyavada | ಧನ್ಯವಾದ | A formal way to say "thank you". |

## Topics Covered
- Kodava festivals and cultural practices (Kailpodhu, Kaveri Sankramana)
- Food and nature (mushrooms, figs, rivers)
- History and geography (Kodagu state, `naadu` regions)
- Traditional architecture (`ainumane`, `kakkattu`, door direction)
- Social structure (`okka` clans, `thakkangaLu`)
- General vocabulary (numbers, directions, questions)
