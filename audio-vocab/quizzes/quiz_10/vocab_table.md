# Quiz 10 — Kodava Vocabulary
> **Source:** quiz_10.mp3
> **Format:** Kodava Takk quiz — 10 questions on culture, history, language, and geography

| English | Kodava Takk | Kannada Script | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Learning / Study | Padippu | ಪಡಿಪು | Kodava word for learning or study; name of the producing team. |
| Team's / Of the team | Dhandathara | ತಂಡತರಾ | Genitive form of "dhanda" (team); "of the team." |
| Presentation | prastuthi | ಪ್ರಸ್ತುತಿ | Borrowed from Kannada/Sanskrit; a formal presentation. |
| Dynasty / Lineage | raajavamsha | ರಾಜವಂಶ | Royal dynasty; Q1 answer: Haleri or Ikkeri dynasty ruled Kodagu. |
| Haleri | Haaleari | ಹಾಲೇರಿ | The Haleri dynasty, the last ruling dynasty of Kodagu. |
| Ikkeri | Ikkeri | ಇಕ್ಕೇರಿ | The Ikkeri (Nayaka) dynasty, earlier rulers of Kodagu. |
| Name | pedha | ಪೆದ | Name of a person or place; Q2 asks whose name was given to a circle. |
| Circle / Junction | chauki | ಚೌಕಿ | A road circle or junction; the bus stand circle in Madikeri. |
| Comic Story Tradition | Naade Kaako | ನಾಡೆ ಕಾಕೋ | Puttari tradition: villagers of a naadu gather to narrate a comic story. |
| Festival | namme | ನಮ್ಮೆ | A festival or celebration; used in "hockey namme" (Kodava Hockey Festival). |
| Family / Clan | vakka | ವಕ್ಕ | A Kodava family or clan; Q4 asks which vakka started the hockey festival. |
| Paandanda | Paandanda | ಪಾಂಡಂಡ | The Paandanda okka, credited with initiating the Kodava Hockey Festival. |
| Pattoale Palame | Pattoale Palame | ಪಟ್ಟೋಲೆ ಪಳಮೆ | Classic Kodava literary work; Q5 asks who translated it to English. |
| Translation | bhaashaanuvaadha | ಭಾಷಾನುವಾದ | The act of translating from one language to another. |
| Capsicum | Boddemelu | ಬೊಡ್ಡೆಮಳು | Kodava word for capsicum/bell pepper; alternate form: Bondemelu. |
| Capsicum (alternate) | Bondemelu | ಬೊಂಡೆಮಳು | Alternate Kodava word for capsicum; both forms are accepted. |
| Ummathaattu | Ummathaattu | ಉಮ್ಮತ್ತಾಟ್ | Traditional Kodava women's folk dance, performed with songs (paattu). |
| Kaveri Sankramana | Changrandi | ಚಂಗ್ರಾಂದಿ | Tula Sankranti; the day the Kaveri river is believed to rise at Talakaveri. |
| Song | paattu | ಪಾಟ್ | A song; specifically an Ummathaattu song in Q7. |
| Thinga Tholeyaarida Sankramana Thandu | Thinga tholeyaarida sankramana thandu | ತಿಂಗ ತೊಳೆಯಾರಿಡ ಸಂಕ್ರಮಣ ತಂದ್ | Ummathaattu song title describing the Kaveri Sankramana event. |
| Highest Mountain | ballya kundhu | ಬಲ್ಯ ಕುಂದ್ | Literally "big mountain"; used to ask for the highest peak in Kodagu. |
| Tadiandamolu | Tadiandamolu | ತಡಿಯಂಡಮೋಳ್ | The highest peak in Kodagu district. |
| Family / Clan | okka | ಒಕ್ಕ | A patrilineal Kodava family or clan unit. |
| Family Head | Pattedhaara | ಪಟ್ಟೆದಾರ | The senior-most head of a Kodava okka; holds a position of honour. |
| New Year | pudhiya kaala | ಪುದಿಯ ಕಾಲ | Literally "new time/era"; the Kodava new year. |
| Edamyaaru | Edamyaaru | ಎಡಮ್ಯಾರ್ | The first month of the Kodava calendar; new year starts April 14. |

## Topics Covered
- Kodava history and dynasties (Haleri, Ikkeri)
- Cultural traditions (Puttari, Naade Kaako, Ummathaattu)
- Kodava literature (Pattoale Palame)
- Geography (Tadiandamolu, Madikeri)
- Social structure (okka, Pattedhaara)
- Calendar and festivals (Edamyaaru, Changrandi, hockey namme)
- Vocabulary (Boddemelu/Bondemelu for capsicum)
