# Quiz 17 — Kodava Vocabulary
> **Source:** quiz_17.mp3
> **Format:** Kodava Takk quiz — 10 questions on Kaveri Changrandi, Puttari, and Kailpodh festivals

| English | Kodava Takk | Kannada Script | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Kodava studies / learning | Padippu | ಪಡಿಪ್ಪು | Study or learning; name of the educational channel. |
| The team's | Thandathira | ತಂಡತ್ರ | Possessive form of 'Thanda' (team). |
| Presentation | prastuti | ಪ್ರಸ್ತುತಿ | Borrowed from Sanskrit; a presentation or offering. |
| Kaveri Changrandi | Kaveri Changrandi | ಕಾವೇರಿ ಚಂಗ್ರಾಂದಿ | Kaveri Sankramana festival; celebrated on Tholeyaar 1. |
| In which month | eed thingath | ಏದ್ ತಿಂಗತ್ | Interrogative phrase: 'which month'. |
| Is celebrated / happens | nadapa | ನಡಪ | Third-person present: 'takes place / is observed'. |
| Tholeyaar (Tula month) | Tholeyaaru | ತೊಳೆಯಾರು | The Kodava name for the month of Tula (October). |
| One / first | ondu | ಒಂದು | The number one; here 'the first day'. |
| Going to Talakaveri | Talakaverik poopa | ತಲಕಾವೇರಿಕ್ ಪೋಪ | Pilgrimage to Talakaveri on Changrandi day. |
| Coconut | thenga | ತೆಂಗ | Coconut; carried as an offering to Talakaveri. |
| Betel nut (one) | berchaaru ondu | ಬೆರ್ಚಾರು ಒಂದು | One betel nut; part of the ritual offering. |
| Custom / tradition | paddhati | ಪದ್ಧತಿ | A practice or tradition; borrowed from Sanskrit. |
| Kiru Changrandi | Kiru Changrandi | ಕಿರು ಚಂಗ್ರಾಂದಿ | 'Small Sankramana'; the day before Kaveri Changrandi. |
| Overflowed | okki parivaka | ಒಕ್ಕಿ ಪರಿವಕ | Verb phrase: 'welled up and flowed'. |
| To stop her | tadipak | ತಡಿಪಕ್ | Infinitive: 'to stop / to block'. |
| Kodava women | Kodava pommakkada | ಕೊಡವ ಪೊಮ್ಮಕ್ಕಡ | 'Kodava women'; pommakka = woman. |
| Pleats of saree | neri | ನೇರಿ | The pleats of a saree; turned backwards at Balamuri. |
| Turned back | bai'k thiriye | ಬೈಕ್ ತಿರಿಯೇ | 'Turned to the back'; mythological event at Balamuri. |
| Balamuri | Balambeeri | ಬಲಂಬೇರಿ | Place in Kodagu where Kaveri's pleats legend originates. |
| Cucumber | chauthe | ಚೌತೆ | Cucumber; used as a vessel for Kaveri's form in Kani Pooje. |
| Silver vessel | illenge boLLari | ಇಲ್ಲೆಂಗೆ ಬೊಳ್ಳರಿ | A silver vessel; used to represent Kaveri in Kani Pooje. |
| Theertha pooje | theertha pooje | ತೀರ್ಥ ಪೂಜೆ | Sacred water worship; performed during Kaveri Changrandi. |
| Kani Pooje | Kani pooje | ಕಣಿ ಪೂಜೆ | 'Pooja of the first sight'; ritual giving Kaveri's form to an object. |
| Nere (sacred bunch) | Nere | ನೆರೆ | Sacred bundle of leaves tied for Kaveri Changrandi. |
| Kummi leaf | Kummi ele | ಕುಮ್ಮಿ ಎಲೆ | A sacred leaf used in the Nere bundle. |
| Gek leaf | gek ele | ಗೇಕ್ ಎಲೆ | Kakai tree leaf; one of the Nere bundle components. |
| Banyan leaf | aal ele | ಆಲ್ ಎಲೆ | Banyan leaf; one of the Nere bundle components. |
| EnjooLi vine | enjooLi kodi | ಎಂಜೂಳಿ ಕೊಡಿ | A vine included in the Nere bundle. |
| Achchi tree creeper | achchinaar baLLi | ಅಚ್ಚಿನಾರ್ ಬಳ್ಳಿ | Creeper used to tie the Nere bundle together. |
| Puttari | Puttari | ಪುತ್ತರಿ | Kodava harvest festival; celebrated in Margali month. |
| One week before | oru vaarak minya | ಒರು ವಾರಕ್ ಮಿಞ | 'One week before'; minya = before/prior. |
| Moonlight | nelchi buLi | ನೆಲ್ಚಿ ಬುಳಿ | Moonlight; villagers practice Kolata under the moon. |
| Kolata (stick dance) | kolaat | ಕೋಲಾಟ್ | Traditional Kodava stick dance performed at Puttari. |
| Practice | padipa | ಪಡಿಪ | To practice / rehearse; here for Kolata. |
| Eed (practice session) | Eed | ಈಡ್ | The pre-Puttari practice period for Kolata. |
| Kailpodh | Kailpoodh | ಕೈಲ್ಪೂದ್ | Kodava festival equivalent to Ayudha Pooja / Navaratri. |
| Plantain leaf | kodibaaLe ele | ಕೊಡಿಬಾಳೆ ಎಲೆ | Plantain leaf used as a plate for ritual offerings. |
| Turmeric powder | manjapodi | ಮಂಜಪೊಡಿ | Turmeric powder; ingredient in Berumbuttu. |
| Jaggery | bella | ಬೆಲ್ಲ | Jaggery; sweetener in ritual foods. |
| Ritual offering | ede | ಎಡೆ | Food offering placed for deities or ancestors. |
| Berumbuttu | Berumbuttu | ಬೆರುಂಬುಟ್ಟು | Ritual steamed cake made on Kailpodh day. |
| Ayudha Pooje | aayuddha pooje | ಆಯುದ್ಧ ಪೂಜೆ | Worship of weapons and tools; performed on Kailpodh. |
| After Puttari | Puttari kainjith | ಪುತ್ತರಿ ಕೈಂಜಿತ್ | 'After Puttari'; kainjith = having finished/after. |
| Seven or eight days | eL att naaL | ಏಳ್ ಅಟ್ಟ್ ನಾಳ್ | 'Seven or eight days'; eL = seven, att = eight. |
| House to house | mane manek | ಮನೆ ಮನೆಕ್ | 'From house to house'; mane = house, 'k = dative. |
| Singing songs | paat paaduva | ಪಾಟ್ ಪಾಡುವ | 'Singing songs'; paat = song, paaduva = to sing. |
| Mane Paado | Mane paado | ಮನೆ ಪಾಡೊ | Custom of singing at homes after Puttari; 7-8 days. |
| Before the meal | umbak minya | ಉಂಬಕ್ ಮಿಞ | 'Before eating'; umbak = to eat (dative), minya = before. |
| Ealakki puttu | eaLakki putt | ಏಳಕ್ಕಿ ಪುಟ್ಟ | Seven-ingredient steamed cake made before Puttari meal. |
| Seven ingredients | eaL padartha | ಏಳ್ ಪದಾರ್ಥ | The seven items in Ealakki puttu. |
| Rice flour | Akkipodi | ಅಕ್ಕಿಪೊಡಿ | Rice flour; base ingredient of Ealakki puttu. |
| Sesame seeds | aLL | ಅಳ್ಳ್ | Sesame seeds; one of the seven Ealakki puttu ingredients. |
| Ginger piece | shunti choor | ಶುಂಟಿ ಚೂರ್ | A piece of ginger; one of the seven ingredients. |
| Bitter gourd piece | kaipake choor | ಕೈಪಕೆ ಚೂರ್ | A piece of bitter gourd; one of the seven ingredients. |
| Coconut piece | thenge choor | ತೆಂಗೆ ಚೂರ್ | A piece of coconut; one of the seven ingredients. |
| Banana | baaLe | ಬಾಳೆ | Banana; one of the seven Ealakki puttu ingredients. |
| Seven small stones | eaL cheriya kall | ಏಳ್ ಚೆರಿಯ ಕಲ್ಲ್ | Seven small stones; symbolic seventh ingredient in puttu. |
| Please | Dayavittu | ದಯವಿಟ್ಟು | Polite request word; borrowed from Kannada. |
| And | pinya | ಪಿಞ | Conjunction meaning 'and / also / then'. |
| Thank you | Dhanyavaada | ಧನ್ಯವಾದ | Expression of gratitude; borrowed from Sanskrit/Kannada. |
