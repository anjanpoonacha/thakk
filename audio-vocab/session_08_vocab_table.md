# Session 08 — Kodava Thakk Vocabulary Table

## Introductory Phrases

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Today we will speak more in Kodava takk. | Indu nanga Kodava thakk'le jaasti thakk pariyana. | `Indu`=today, `nanga`=we, `jaasti`=more, `thakk pariyana`=will speak. |
| Now we should be understanding most of it. | Ikka sumaar ella artha aapa'la nangakku. | `Ikka`=now, `sumaar ella`=mostly all, `artha aapa'la`=will be understood, `nangakku`=to us. |

## Present Progressive Tense

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Do you remember what all we noted in session seven? | Ikka nanga session seven'l entha'ella noatichi gyaana uNda? | Question form. |
| What are you doing? | Ninga entha maadiyand uLLira? | Present progressive, 2nd person plural/formal. |
| What are they doing? | Ainga entha maadiyand uNdu? | Present progressive, 3rd person plural. |
| For singing, what would one say? | Paattu paaduwa'nga entha oNNu? | Question about usage. |
| We are singing. | Nanga paadiyand uNdu. | `Nanga` = We, `paadiyand uNdu` = are singing. |
| I am singing. | Naanu paadiyand uLLo. | `Naanu` = I, `paadiyand uLLo` = am singing. |
| For dancing, what would one say? | Aaduwa'nga entha oNNu? | Question about usage. |
| She is dancing. | Ava aadiyand uNdu. | `Ava` = She, `aadiyand uNdu` = is dancing. |
| They are dancing. | Ainga aadiyand uNdu. | `Ainga` = They, `aadiyand uNdu` = are dancing. |
| For pushing? | Noakkuwa'k? | Question about usage. |
| We are pushing a car. | Nanga oru kaatt'ina noakyand uNdu. | `Nanga`=we, `kaatt'ina`=car (object), `noakyand uNdu`=are pushing. |
| He is pushing a car. | Avan oru kaatt'ina noakyand uNdu. | `Avan` = He. |
| For peeling? | Maanduw'k entha oNNu? | Question about usage. |
| This cat is peeling. | Ii puunye maandyand uNdu. | `puunye`=cat, `maandyand uNdu`=is peeling. |
| What is it peeling? | Entha'na maandyand uNdu? | Question form. |
| It is peeling a banana. | BaaLe'na maandyand uNdu. | `BaaLe'na`=banana (object). |
| This cat is peeling a banana. | Ii puunye baaLe'na maandyand uNdu. | Example sentence. |
| For flying? | Paaruwa'k? | Question about usage. |
| This bird is flying. | Ii miga paaryand uNdu. | `miga`=bird, `paaryand uNdu`=is flying. |
| For biting? | Kadippuwa'k? | Question about usage. |
| (It) is biting. | Kadchond uNdu. | Verb `kadippuwa` (to bite) in present progressive. |
| He is biting. | Avan kadchond uNdu. | Example sentence. |
| For playing? | Kalippuw'k entha oNNu? | Question about usage. |
| (They) are playing. | Kalchond uNdu. | Verb `kalippuwa` (to play) in present progressive. |
| What are they doing? | Ainga entha maadiyand uNdu? | Question form. |
| How many people are there? | Ainga echchak aaL uNdu? | `echchak`=how many, `aaL`=people. |
| Two people are playing. | Ainga dhand aaLu kalchond uNdu. | `dhand aaLu`=two people. |
| For drinking? | Kudippuwa'k? | Question about usage. |
| (She) is drinking. | Kudchond uNdu. | Verb `kudippuwa` (to drink) in present progressive. |
| She is drinking water. | Ava niiru kudchond uNdu. | `niiru`=water. |
| She is drinking coffee. | Ava kaapi kudchond uNdu. | `kaapi`=coffee. |
| Coffee | Kaapi | Kodava word for coffee. |
| For chasing? | Balippuwa'k? | Question about usage. |
| (It) is chasing. | Balchond uNdu. | Verb `balippuwa` (to chase) in present progressive. |
| This bear is chasing it. | Ii karadi adh'na balchond uNdu. | `karadi`=bear, `adh'na`=it (object). |
| He is going to the right. | Iva baltha barii'k poayand uNdu. | `baltha barii'k`=to the right, `poayand uNdu`=is going. |

## Arithmetic Drills

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Seventy | êLuvadu | Number 70. |
| Seventy-one | êLuvath ondh | Number 71. |
| Things | Saamaana | Vocabulary. |
| 10 plus 61 things became 71. | Pathth pinja aruvath ondh saamaana êLuvath ondh aachi. | `pathth`=10, `pinja`=plus, `aruvath ondh`=61, `aachi`=became. |
| 20 plus 52 marbles became 72 marbles. | Iruvadu pinja aimbadh dhand goali... êLuvadh dhand goali aachi. | `iruvadu`=20, `aimbadh dhand`=52, `goali`=marbles. |
| When 30 and 43 are added it became 73. | Nuppadu enja naapadh muundh uruppu kuudunaka... êLuvath muundh. | `nuppadu`=30, `naapadh muundh`=43, `kuudunaka`=when added. |
| 40 plus 34 became 74. | Naapadu pinja nuppath naal soLLe... êLuvath naal. | `naapadu`=40, `nuppath naal`=34. |
| Honey bees | Thenu valanji | Vocabulary. |
| When 50 plus 25 are added it became 75. | Aimbadu pinja iruvath anjii kuudunaka, êLuvath anjii aachi. | `aimbadu`=50, `iruvath anjii`=25. |
| When 60 plus 16 grapes are added it became 76 grapes. | Aruvadu pinja padhinaar dhraakshe kuuduvaka êLuvath aar dhraakshe aachi. | `aruvadu`=60, `padhinaar`=16, `dhraakshe`=grapes. |
| Cloves | Lavanga | Vocabulary. |
| When 70 plus 7 cloves are added it became 77. | êLuvadu pinja êêL lavanga kuuduvaka êLuvath êêL aachi. | `êLuvadu`=70, `êêL`=7. |
| When 10 plus 68 are added it became 78. | Pathth pinja aruvath êtt kuru kuudunaka êLuvath êtt aachi. | `pathth`=10, `aruvath êtt`=68. |
| Vegetables | Tharkari | Vocabulary. |
| 20 vegetables plus 59 vegetables became 79. | Iruvadu tharkari pinja aimbath woyimbadh tharkari... êLuvath woyimbadh. | `iruvadu`=20, `aimbath woyimbadh`=59. |
| Eighty | êNbadu | Number 80. |
| Mushrooms | Kummu | Vocabulary. |
| 30 plus 50 mushrooms were added, it became 80 mushrooms. | Nuppadu pinja aimbadu kummu kuudithu êNbadu kummu aachi. | `nuppadu`=30, `aimbadu`=50, `kuudithu`=were added. |

| Open the door. | Padi thora. | Command form. `padi`=door, `thora`=open. |
| Come in. | WoLu'k baa. | Command form. `woLu'k`=inside, `baa`=come. |

| Don't want | BaaNda | Vocabulary. |

## Kaveri Sankramana Cultural Vocabulary

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Kaveri Sankramana is an important festival for Kodavas. | Kaveri Sankramana Kodava'da ballya namme. | `ballya namme`=big/important festival. |
| We Kodavas worship Goddess Kaveri. | Nanga Kodava Devi Kaveri'na puuje maaduva. | `puuje maaduva`=do worship. |
| Which goddess do we Kodavas all worship? | Nanga Kodava ellaaru dhaara'na puuje maaduva? | Rhetorical question. `dhaara'na`=whom. |
| (We) worship Goddess Kaveri. | Devi Kaveri'na puuje maaduva. | Answer to the rhetorical question. |
| Kaveri Sankramana is also known as Kaveri Changrandi. | Kaveri Sankramana'thina Kaveri Changrandi'ndu aNNuva. | `aNNuva`=(they) say/call. |
| River Kaveri originates in Thala Kaveri at the foothills of Brahmagiri hills. | Kaveri poLe Brahmagiri kund'ra adii'l Thala Kaveri'l udhipa. | `poLe`=river, `kund'ra adii'l`=at the foothills, `udhipa`=originates. |
| Kodava people visit Thala Kaveri on Sankramana and offer worship. | Kodava jana Sankramana'k Thala Kaveri'k poythu puuje maaduva. | `jana`=people, `poythu`=having gone. |
| Holy water gushes out in Thala Kaveri on Sankramana day. | Sankramana dhina Thala Kaveri'l Theerthodbhava aapa. | `dhina`=day, `Theerthodbhava`=gushing of holy water, `aapa`=happens. |
| Theerthodbhava happens in the Kodava month of Tholeyaar. | Theerthodbhava Tholeyaar thinga'thra Thula raashi'l aapa. | `thinga`=month, `Thula raashi`=Libra. |
| On that day Mother Earth is worshipped in the home. | Andh mane'l Bhoomi thaayi'na puuje maaduva. | `Andh`=that day, `mane'l`=in the home, `Bhoomi thaayi`=Mother Earth. |
| That puja is called kaNi puuje. | Aa puuje'na kaNi puuje'ndu oNNuva. | `Aa puuje'na`=That puja. |
| Dosas are prepared on Sankramana and offered to our ancestors. | Sankramana namme'l dhose maadithu kaarana'ng'k meedhi beppa. | `kaarana'ng'k`=to ancestors, `meedhi beppa`=food is offered. |
| Posts made with bamboo and creeper (festival installation). | Boththu | Placed near house, cattle shed, and paddy field. |
| For this festival, boththu is pitched near the house, cattle shed, and paddy field. | Ii namme'k mane, kottu, beli'l ella boththu beppa. | `kottu`=cattle shed, `beli`=paddy field. |
| The month of Tholeyaar is auspicious to offer food to our ancestors. | Tholeyaar thinga kaarana'ng'k meedhi beppa'k nalla thinga. | `nalla thinga`=good month. |
