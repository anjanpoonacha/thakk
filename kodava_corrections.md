### Case Errors

- WHAT: `ava'k noatiyand ikkathe!` (FINAL.md line 2038 — "Don't keep staring at her!")
- CORRECT: `avala noatiyand ikkathe!`
- WHY: "Staring at her" requires accusative case (direct object), not dative.
  Equivalent to Kannada: **avalannu** nodkond irabeda (accusative ✓)
  NOT: avalige nodkond irabeda (dative ✗)
  `ava'k` = dative (to her / for her benefit) — wrong for staring
  `avala` = accusative (her, direct object) — correct
- CONFIDENCE: certain

## Fundamental Language Rules (native speaker confirmed)

- RULE: 'da is NEVER used as genitive in Kodava takk. Always use 'ra.
  Examples: Kodagu'ra (not Kodagu'da).
  EXCEPTION: naada (my) is CORRECT — do not change naada to naara.
  CONFIDENCE: certain

- RULE: uNd is rarely used. The correct form is injathe.
  CONFIDENCE: certain

- RULE: Ondh (one) → when used as article "one day" = Oar dhivsa, not Ondh dhivsa
  CONFIDENCE: certain

## Verb: thoLip'k (to laugh)
- thoLipa'k = to laugh (infinitive)
- naa thoLipi = I laughed
- ninga thoLiri = you laughed
- ellaru thoLcha't = everyone laughed
- thoLchathe = laughed (past)
- thoLchand injathe = was laughing (progressive)
- thoLchand und = is laughing (progressive)

## SECTION C: Past Tense Person Forms (Confirmed — FINAL.md + native speaker)

### Past tense VARIES by person

#### Regular -uw'k verbs (past root = progressive - iyand)
| Person | Ending | Example (maaduw'k) | Devanagari |
|---|---|---|---|
| naa | root + unê (spoken) OR root + iye (textbook) | maadunê / maadiye | मादुनॅ / मादिये |
| nii | root + iiyaa | maadiiyaa | मादीया |
| āāwu/ava/adh/ainga | root + ichi | maadichi | मादिचि |
| nanga | root + DDichi (ONLY for maad root) OR root + ichi | maDDichi | माड्डिचि |
| ninga | root + iiraa | maadiiraa | मादीरा |

Note: maDDichi (retroflex DD) is ONLY for the maaduw'k root. Other verbs use plain -ichi for nanga.

#### Irregular -p'k/-b'k verbs
| Person | Ending | bapp'k | poap'k | thimb'k | kudip'k |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| naa | stem + ê | bandhê | poanê | thindhê | kudichê |
| nii | stem + iiyaa | bandhiiyaa | poaiiyaa | thindhiiyaa | kudichiiyaa |
| āāwu/nanga/ainga | 3rd form | baanthê | poachi | thindhêchi | kudichethê |
| ninga | stem + iiraa | bandhiiraa | poaiiraa | thindhiiraa | kudichiiraa |

#### Special case: êêw'k (to wake up)
- naa → êêtê
- āāwu/nanga/ainga → êêdêthê (native speaker confirmed)
