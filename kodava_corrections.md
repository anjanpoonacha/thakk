## SECTION A: Vocabulary Corrections

- WHAT: `hasi` used as hunger / hungry; `naa'k hasi aachi` used for "I am hungry"
- CORRECT: `kela baip'w` = hungry / stomach is growling; `naa'k kela baip'w` = I am hungry; `naa'k kela baichaind'und` = I am hungry (progressive)
- WHY: `hasi` is not the correct Kodava word for hunger. The correct expression uses `kela` (stomach) + `baip'w` (growling/hungry). The textbook also confirms: `kela bayp'k` = to be hungry (lit. for the stomach to growl). `hasi` may be a Kannada borrowing and must not be used.
- FORMS: kela baip'w (state/noun), kela baipa (present/adjective), kela baichaind'und (present progressive)
- USAGE: naa'k kela baip'w = I am hungry. naada kela bayp'le = I'm not hungry (textbook confirmed)
- CONFIDENCE: native speaker confirmed

- WHAT: bappa (incorrectly assumed to mean "father")
- CORRECT: appa
- WHY: bappa in the textbook refers exclusively to the verb "coming" (from bapp'k = to come). The word for father is appa — native speaker confirmed. Do NOT infer bappa = father from the textbook.
- USAGE: niida appa elli uNd? = Where is your father?
- CONFIDENCE: native speaker confirmed

- WHAT: gothth aapa (I miss them)
- CORRECT: ghê-na aapa
- WHY: gothth = knowledge. Missing someone = ghê-na aapa
- CONFIDENCE: certain

- WHAT: mansa used as people or crowd
- CORRECT: jana (people); mansa = individual man — do NOT use mansa for crowd
- CONFIDENCE: certain

- WHAT: aadiyan (laughing)
- CORRECT: tholipo = to laugh; aado = to dance. These are different.
- WHY: aadiyan = dancing, NOT laughing. Laughing = thoLchandh / tholipo
- CONFIDENCE: certain

- WHAT: rasa'da gaaLi uNd (there is an aroma of taste)

- WHAT: ava'l elli aachenge bisi gadhdhala uNd (she always has hot food)

- WHAT: ava baarii chaayi mansa (she is a very good person)

- WHAT: naan (I) past of maaduw'k
- CORRECT: maadunê (spoken) or maadiye (textbook)
- WHY: root + unê (spoken) OR root + iye (textbook)
- CONFIDENCE: verified

- WHAT: nii (you) past of maaduw'k
- CORRECT: maadiiyaa
- WHY: root + iiyaa
- CONFIDENCE: verified

- WHAT: āāwu/ava/adh/ainga (3rd person) past of maaduw'k
- CORRECT: maadichi
- WHY: root + ichi
- CONFIDENCE: verified

- WHAT: nanga (we) past of maaduw'k
- CORRECT: maDDichi
- WHY: root + DDichi — the retroflex DD is ONLY for the maaduw'k root. All other verbs use plain -ichi for nanga.

- WHY: root + iiraa
- CONFIDENCE: verified

- WHY: poap'killê expresses uncertainty about not going — distinct from poap'le which is definite. The ê suffix marks the dubitative mood.
- CONFIDENCE: native speaker confirmed
