### Case Errors

- WHAT: `ava'k noatiyand ikkathe!` (FINAL.md line 2038 — "Don't keep staring at her!")
- CORRECT: `avala noatiyand ikkathe!`
- WHY: "Staring at her" requires accusative case (direct object), not dative.
  Equivalent to Kannada: **avalannu** nodkond irabeda (accusative ✓)
  NOT: avalige nodkond irabeda (dative ✗)
  `ava'k` = dative (to her / for her benefit) — wrong for staring
  `avala` = accusative (her, direct object) — correct
- CONFIDENCE: certain

## Vocabulary Corrections

- WHAT: gothth aapa (I miss them)
- CORRECT: ghê-na aapa
- WHY: gothth = knowledge. Missing someone = ghê-na aapa
- CONFIDENCE: certain

- WHAT: mansa (used as people/crowd)
- CORRECT: jana (people); mansa = individual man — do NOT use mansa for crowd
- CONFIDENCE: certain

- WHAT: aadiyan (laughing)
- CORRECT: tholipo = to laugh; aado = to dance. These are different.
- WHY: aadiyan = dancing, NOT laughing. Laughing = thoLchandh / tholipo
- CONFIDENCE: certain

- WHAT: rasa'da gaaLi uNd (there is an aroma of taste)
- CORRECT: rasa'thra ghamana injathe
- WHY: Correct genitive -'thra and correct verb form
- CONFIDENCE: certain

- WHAT: neyi'da rasa'da gaaLi
- CORRECT: neyi'ra ghama injathe
- WHY: ghama = aroma/smell (not in FINAL.md — native speaker supplied)
- CONFIDENCE: certain

- WHAT: ava'l elli aachenge bisi gadhdhala uNd (she always has hot food)

- CONFIDENCE: certain

- WHAT: ava baarii chaayi mansa (she is a very good person)

- WHAT: mane'l'ella (all over the house)

- CONFIDENCE: certain

- WHAT: injand (free progressive particle used in story)

## Fundamental Language Rules (native speaker confirmed)

- RULE: 'da is NEVER used as genitive in Kodava takk. Always use 'ra.
  Examples: Kodagu'ra (not Kodagu'da).
  EXCEPTION: naada (my) is CORRECT — do not change naada to naara.
  CONFIDENCE: certain

- RULE: uNd is rarely used. The correct form is injathe.
  CONFIDENCE: certain

- RULE: Ondh (one) → when used as article "one day" = Oar dhivsa, not Ondh dhivsa
  CONFIDENCE: certain

## Verb: thoLip'k (to laugh)
- thoLipa'k = to laugh (infinitive)
- naa thoLipi = I laughed
- ninga thoLiri = you laughed
- ellaru thoLcha't = everyone laughed
- thoLchathe = laughed (past)
- thoLchand injathe = was laughing (progressive)
- thoLchand und = is laughing (progressive)
CONFIDENCE: certain (native speaker supplied full conjugation)
