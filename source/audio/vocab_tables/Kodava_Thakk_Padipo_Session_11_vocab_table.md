# Kodava Thakk Padipo — Session 11 Vocabulary Table

**Series:** Kodava Thakk Padipo (Learn Kodava Language) by Kodava Koota
**Session:** 11
**Topics:** Past tense of ipp'k, past progressive, comparatives, superlatives, place vocabulary, household verbs, past tense of maaduw'k

---

## Introductory Phrases

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Kodava language | Kodava thakk | The name of the language |
| Shall we learn Kodava language again? | Nanga Kodava thakk puna padikana? | nanga = we, puna = again, padikana = shall we learn? |
| This is session eleven. | Session eleven ika. | ika = this is |
| Let's revise what was in session ten. | Session ten il aana na nanga revise maadana. | il aana na = what was in, nanga = we, revise maadana = let's revise |
| Let's do one recap. | Oar recap maadana. | oar = one, maadana = let's do |
| Okay? | Acha? | Common word for 'okay' or 'alright' |
| Now | Ippa | Adverb of time |

## Past Tense of ipp'k (to be)

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| to be | ipp'k | Infinitive form of the verb 'to be' |
| I was at home. | Naan mane'l injie. | naan = I, mane'l = at home, injie = was (1st person singular past) |
| You were in your room. | Neen niida koambri'l injia. | neen = you (sg.), niida = your, koambri'l = in the room, injia = were (2nd person singular past) |
| She was in the backyard. | Ava patti'l injath. | ava = she, patti'l = in the backyard, injath = was (3rd person singular past) |
| We were in the kitchen. | Nanga adige mane'l injath. | nanga = we, adige mane'l = in the kitchen, injath = were (1st person plural past) |
| You (pl.) were in school. | Ninga school'il injira. | ninga = you (pl./formal), school'il = in school, injira = were (2nd person plural past) |
| They were at work. | Ainga kelsath'l injath. | ainga = they, kelsath'l = at work, injath = were (3rd person plural past) |

## Past Progressive Tense

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| I was going home. | Naan mane'k poyoNd injie. | naan = I, mane'k = to home, poyoNd = going (progressive participle), injie = was (past of ipp'k, 1st person) |

## Comparisons & Superlatives

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| a lot, more | jaasthi | Denotes a large quantity |
| a few, less | kammi | Denotes a small quantity |
| even more, still more | innye | Adds emphasis; innye jaasthi = even more |
| even more | innye jaasthi | Combination meaning 'even more' |
| even less | innye kammi | Combination meaning 'even less' |
| enough | madhi | |
| This much is enough. | Ichchak madhi. | ichchak = this much, madhi = enough |
| Do you want even more? | Nikku innye jaasthi boNduva? | nikku = for you, innye jaasthi = even more, boNduva = do you want? |
| No. I want even less. | Ille. Naak innye kammi boNdu. | naak = for me, innye kammi = even less, boNdu = want |
| more than X | X'kinji | Comparative suffix: X'kinji = than X |
| That house is bigger than my car. | Aa mane naada kaar'kinji ballyadh. | aa mane = that house, naada kaar = my car, 'kinji = than, ballyadh = is big |
| My car is smaller than that house. | Naada kaar aa mane'kinji cheriyadh. | naada kaar = my car, aa mane'kinji = than that house, cheriyadh = is small |
| This apple is redder than the mango. | Ee aappil aa maange'kinji choondadh. | ee aappil = this apple, aa maange'kinji = than that mango, choondadh = is red |
| This banana is yellower than that apple. | Ee baaLe aa aappil'kinji manja. | ee baaLe = this banana, aa aappil'kinji = than that apple, manja = is yellow |
| (is) yellow | manja | Adjective for yellow — does NOT take -dh suffix (unlike choondadh) |
| (is) red | choondadh | Adjective for red — takes -dh suffix |
| more than everything | ellaang'inji | Superlative marker: ellaang = everything, 'inji = than |
| more than everybody | ellaark'inji | Superlative for people: ellaark = everybody, 'inji = than |
| That house is bigger than everything else. | Aa mane ellaang'inji ballyadh. | aa mane = that house, ellaang'inji = than everything, ballyadh = is big |
| The apple is redder than everything else. | Aappil ellaang'inji choondadh. | |
| The banana is yellower than everything else. | BaaLe ellaang'inji manja. | |
| She has work. | Ivak kelsa uNd. | ivak = she has |
| He doesn't have work. | Avang kelsa ille. | avang = he, kelsa ille = no work |
| They don't have work. | Aing'k kelsa ille. / Aink kelsa ille. | Two variations for 'they don't have work' |
| She has more work than him. | Ivak avang'inji jaasthi kelsa uNd. | ivak = she has, avang'inji = than him, jaasthi kelsa uNd = has more work |
| She does more work than him. | Iva avang'inji jaasthi kelsa maaduva. | iva = she, avang'inji = than him, jaasthi kelsa maaduva = does more work |
| She does more work than the boy. | Iva kinnang'inji jaasthi kelsa maaduva. | kinnang'inji = than the boy |
| She has more work than everybody else. | Ivak ellaark'inji jaasthi kelsa uNd. | ellaark'inji = than everybody |
| She is doing more work than everybody else. | Iva ellaark'inji jaasthi kelsa maadiyoNd uNd. | |

## Place Vocabulary

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| store, shop | angadi | General term for any shop |
| My older sister went to the store. | Naada akka angadi'k poochi. | naada akka = my older sister, angadi'k = to the store, poochi = went |
| hospital | aaspathre | |
| He is going to the hospital because he has a leg pain. | Avang kaal nambale injaang aaspathre'k poyoNd uNd. | avang = he, kaal nambale = leg pain, injaang = because there was, aaspathre'k = to the hospital, poyoNd uNd = is going |
| forest | kaad | |
| The forest has lions. | Kaad'l simma uNd. | kaad'l = in the forest, simma = lion(s), uNd = has/are present |
| lake | kere | |
| Did you go to swim in the lake? | Neen kere'l meendokku poya? | neen = you (sg.), kere'l = in the lake, meendokku = to swim, poya = did you go? |
| Are you going swimming in the lake? | Neen kere'l meendokku poppiya? | poppiya = are you going? |
| riverside | poLekare | poLe = river, kare = edge/bank |
| river | poLe | |
| bank, edge | kare | |
| We found many flowers by the riverside. | Nangak poLekare'l dhumba poo kittichi. | nangak = for us/we, poLekare'l = by the riverside, dhumba = many, poo = flowers, kittichi = got/found |
| sea | samudra | |
| The sea is scary to look at. | Samudra nootuwak poodi aappa. | nootuwak = to look at, poodi aappa = is scary |
| wedding hall | mangala mane | mangala = wedding, mane = house |
| Their wedding is at the wedding hall. | Avara mangala mangala mane'l aapa. | avara mangala = their wedding, mangala mane'l = at the wedding hall, aapa = happens/is |
| ancestral home | balyamane | |
| My brothers are playing in the ancestral home. | Naada anna thamma balyamane'l kalchoNd uNd. | naada anna thamma = my older and younger brothers, balyamane'l = in the ancestral home, kalchoNd uNd = are playing |
| temple | devasthana | Also spoken as davasthana in colloquial speech |
| Let us go to the temple and come back. | Devasthana'k poith bakana. | devasthana'k = to the temple, poith bakana = let's go and come |
| We went to the temple for the festival. | Nanga namme'k devasthana'k poochi. | nanga = we, namme'k = for the festival, devasthana'k = to the temple, poochi = went |
| coffee estate | kaapi thota | kaapi = coffee, thota = estate/garden |
| We picked coffee in the coffee estate. | Nanga kaapi thota'th kaapi kojjath. | nanga = we, kaapi thota'th = in the coffee estate, kaapi kojjath = picked coffee |
| field (paddy field) | belu | |
| field work | bel pani | pani = work |
| Let's go for field work. | Bel pani'k pokana. | bel pani'k = to the field work, pokana = let's go |
| Paddy is grown in the field. | Bel'l nellu boLthuva. | bel'l = in the field, nellu = paddy, boLthuva = is grown |
| paddy | nellu | Unhusked rice |

## Household Verbs

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| to wash (dishes, hands — not clothes) | kathuw'k | Used for hands, vessels, dishes |
| to wash hands | kai kathuw'k | kai = hand |
| to wash dishes | paathra kathuw'k | paathra = vessel/dish |
| Wash the dishes. (imperative) | paathra katho. | Imperative form of kathuw'k |
| Wash your hands. (imperative) | kai katho. | Imperative form of kathuw'k |
| to wash clothes | volip'k | Exclusively for washing clothes — not for dishes or hands |
| Wash the clothes. (imperative) | batte volipa. | batte = clothes; imperative form of volip'k |
| to sweep | vodukkuw'k | To sweep the house |
| to fold clothes | madukkuw'k | |
| to pour | bookuw'k | |
| to pour water | neer bookuw'k | neer = water |
| to pluck | koyyuw'k | |
| to pluck flowers | poo koyyuw'k | poo = flower |
| to cook, to make food | adige maaduw'k | adige = cooking/food, maaduw'k = to do/make |

## Past Tense of maaduw'k (to do/make)

| English | Kodava Takk | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| I cooked food the other day. | Monyaandh naan adike maadiye. | monyaandh = the other day, naan = I, adike maadiye = cooked food; maadiye = 1st person sg. past |
| Yesterday, we all had fun. | Ninnaandh nanga ellaru majaa maadichi. | ninnaandh = yesterday, nanga ellaru = we all, majaa = fun, maadichi = did (plural past) |
| Did you do well in school? | Neen school'ile chaayil maadiya? | neen = you (sg.), school'ile = in school, chaayil = well, maadiya = did you do? (2nd person sg. past) |
| When did you (pl.) fight? | Ninga dhandaL ekka jagala maadiye? / Ninga dhandaL ekka jagala maadira? | ninga dhandaL = you (pl.), ekka = when, jagala = fight; both maadiye and maadira are valid 2nd person plural past forms |
| On Monday, he ironed his clothes. | ThingaLaache avan avan'ra batte isthri maadichi. | thingaLaache = on Monday, avan = he, avan'ra batte = his clothes, isthri = iron, maadichi = did (3rd person past) |
| Last night, two birds made a lot of noise. | Ninnaandh bayt dhand pakshi sumaar sadhdh maadichi. | ninnaandh bayt = last night, dhand pakshi = two birds, sumaar = a lot, sadhdh = noise, maadichi = made |
| Did you do your work? | Neen niida kelsa maadiya? | neen = you (sg.), niida kelsa = your work, maadiya = did you do? |
| No, I still haven't done it. | Ille, naan innu maadithle. | naan = I, innu = still/yet, maadithle = have not done |
| Shall I cook tonight? | Naan indh bayt adike maaduvoNduva? | naan = I, indh bayt = tonight, adike = food, maaduvoNduva = is it needed to cook? |
| No. You don't have to do anything. | Ille. Neen ondhu maadoNda. | neen = you (sg.), ondhu = anything, maadoNda = don't have to do |
